Common Termite Problems and How We Solve Them

Understanding and Addressing Termite Infestations

Dealing with termites can be overwhelming, largely because they are often undetected until significant damage has occurred. Recognizing the common signs and understanding the types of problems they cause is the first step towards effective termite control. At Elgin Termite, we have extensive experience dealing with a wide range of termite-related issues in Bartlett, IL, and we are equipped to provide tailored solutions for each scenario.

Subterranean Termite Activity

Subterranean termites are the most common and often the most destructive type in our area. They live in underground colonies and build mud tubes to travel between their colony and their food source – your home's wooden structure. These mud tubes are one of the most identifiable signs of a subterranean termite infestation. They protect the termites from predators and the elements as they travel from the soil to the wood. Finding these tubes, especially on your foundation walls, crawl spaces, or even interior walls, is a strong indicator of their presence. If you discover these tubes, it's crucial to contact a professional immediately. We will meticulously trace these tubes to locate the colony and apply targeted treatments that not only eliminate the current infestations but also establish a protective barrier around your home to prevent future invasion. Our methods are designed to disrupt their pathways and eliminate the colony at its source, ensuring long-term protection.

Wood Damage and Hollow Sounds

Termites consume cellulose, which is found in wood. As they eat the wood from the inside out, it becomes weakened and can sound hollow when tapped. Often, the surface of the wood may appear normal, masking the extensive damage within. This can be particularly alarming as significant structural integrity can be compromised before visual cues on the exterior become apparent. Common areas where this damage might be noticed include door frames, window sills, baseboards, and even furniture. If you notice any areas of wood that feel soft, appear darkened, or sound hollow when you tap on them, it's important to investigate further, or better yet, call in the experts. Our technicians are skilled in identifying the subtle signs of wood damage caused by termites, including the distinct patterns of their tunneling. We use specialized tools like moisture meters and boroscopes to detect hidden damage without causing unnecessary disturbance to your property. Once termite damage is confirmed, we develop a treatment plan that focuses on eradicating the colony responsible for the damage and we can also recommend steps for repairing the affected wood. Addressing the active infestation is paramount to preventing further deterioration of your home's structure. We partner with trusted contractors for significant structural repairs if needed, ensuring a seamless process for you. The extent of the damage often dictates the complexity of the repair, emphasizing the need for early detection.

Discarded Termite Wings

Termites undergo a swarming period, typically in the warmer months, when reproductive termites (swarmers or alates) leave the colony to establish new ones. During this process, they shed their wings after finding a mate and a suitable location to start a new colony. Finding small, translucent wings scattered near windowsills, doorways, or light fixtures, usually indoors, is a definitive sign of a termite swarm having occurred, indicating an active infestation is likely somewhere close by. These wings are often mistaken for flying ants, but a closer look reveals they are uniform in size and shape, unlike ant wings. A swarm is essentially a colony's way of expanding, so seeing discarded wings means a mature colony is present and attempting to reproduce. If you see these wings, it's not just a fleeting nuisance; it's a clear warning sign. We conduct comprehensive inspections to locate the source of the swarm and the main colony. Our treatment plans are then designed to target the established colony and prevent future swarming activities. Understanding the swarming season in Bartlett, IL allows us to be particularly vigilant during these times and offer preventative measures to homeowners. The presence of these wings is a significant red flag that should never be ignored.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, while less common in Illinois than subterranean termites, produce small, pellet-like fecal droppings called frass. These droppings are often the only visible sign of a drywood termite infestation. They are typically found in small piles near infested wood, sometimes resembling sawdust or coarse sand. The color of the frass can vary depending on the type of wood the termites are consuming. Unlike subterranean termites that live underground, drywood termites live entirely within the wood they infest, making their detection more challenging. The frass is essentially ejected from small holes in the wood as they tunnel and consume it. If you notice small piles of what looks like sawdust in areas around wooden furniture, beams, or walls, it's important to examine it closely. Disturbing these piles can sometimes reveal active termites boring small holes nearby. While drywood termites are not as prevalent as subterranean termites in Bartlett, IL, they can still cause significant damage if left unchecked. Our technicians are trained to identify the specific signs of drywood termites and employ targeted treatments, such as spot treatments directly into the infested wood using specialized termiticides. We carefully assess the extent of the drywood infestation to determine the most effective and least invasive treatment method. Early detection of frass can prevent widespread damage and the need for more extensive repairs.

Buckling Paint or Bubbling Walls

While often associated with water damage, buckling paint or bubbling wallpaper can also be a sign of termites tunneling just beneath the surface. As termites consume the wood or cardboard beneath the paint or wallpaper, they create voids and moisture that cause the surface to distort. Termites require moisture to survive, and their activity can create humid conditions within the walls, leading to these visual cues. These aesthetic issues might seem minor at first glance, but they can be indicative of a much larger underlying problem. Don't dismiss these visual anomalies simply as painting issues or minor water leaks. Investigating the cause is crucial. Our inspectors know to look beyond the surface and can differentiate between water damage and termite damage based on their experience and specialized tools. We may use moisture meters to detect elevated moisture levels within the walls or thermal imaging to identify areas of heat generated by termite activity. If termites are found to be the cause, our treatment will focus on eliminating the colony and addressing the underlying moisture issue that may have attracted them in the first place. Repairing the cosmetic damage becomes secondary to eradicating the pests causing it. Recognizing these subtle signs can lead to early detection and prevent the infestation from spreading further within the wall cavities. Homeowners should be alert to any unexplained changes in the appearance of their walls or paintwork.

Swarming Termites

Seeing a swarm of winged insects emerging from your home, particularly in the spring or after a rain shower, is a sure sign of an active termite infestation. These are the reproductive termites leaving the colony to find a new location to establish their own. While the swarm itself may be temporary, it indicates the presence of a mature, established colony nearby. Swarming termites are often mistaken for flying ants, but there are key differences in their body shape and wing structure that our experts can easily identify. Swarming is the most visible manifestation of a termite problem and should be taken seriously. It is a signal that the colony has grown to a significant size and is actively reproducing. If you witness a termite swarm on or around your property, do not ignore it. Contact us immediately. We can schedule an emergency inspection to confirm the presence of termites and initiate a rapid response to prevent further damage. Capturing a sample of the swarming insects can be helpful for positive identification. Our immediate action can prevent secondary infestations from being established. Swarms occurring indoors are particularly concerning as it means the mature colony is located within your home's structure.

Termite Activity Following Rain

Termites are attracted to moisture, and increased activity can often be observed after rain. Wet soil provides ideal conditions for subterranean termites to move and construct their mud tubes. Humid conditions following rainfall can also trigger swarming events. If you notice an increase in termite-related signs, such as new mud tubes or swarming activity, after a period of rain, it reinforces the likelihood of an active infestation. Environmental factors play a significant role in termite behavior and can sometimes make previously hidden infestations more apparent. Monitoring your property for signs of termites, especially after periods of wet weather, is a good preventative measure. Our inspection services are particularly valuable after heavy rain, as this is often when termite activity becomes more noticeable. We can assess how moisture levels are impacting potential termite habitats around your home. Understanding the link between moisture and termite activity helps us to identify vulnerable areas of your property and recommend targeted preventative measures. Proper drainage and addressing leaky pipes or faucets can significantly reduce the attractiveness of your home to termites. Simple landscaping adjustments can also help divert water away from your foundation.

Termite Pellets on Windowsills

Similar to frass, drywood termites can sometimes push their fecal pellets out of tiny kick-out holes onto horizontal surfaces below, including windowsills. These small, hard pellets are a distinctive sign of a drywood termite infestation. Unlike the powdery residue of some other pests, termite pellets are uniform in size and six-sided when viewed under magnification. Finding these pellets on windowsills or other surfaces within your home should prompt immediate investigation. It's a clear indication that drywood termites are present and actively consuming the wood nearby. While less common than subterranean termites in our service area, drywood termites can cause significant damage to furniture, structural timbers, and other wooden elements within your home. Our inspection will focus on locating the source of these pellets and identifying the infested wood. Treatment for drywood termites typically involves localized treatments or fumigation, depending on the severity and extent of the infestation. We will determine the most appropriate treatment method based on the specific circumstances of your infestation. Prompt identification of termite pellets is essential to prevent the infestation from spreading to other wooden structures in your home. Regular cleaning of windowsills and other vulnerable areas can sometimes help to uncover these early signs of infestation.

Damaged Wood that Crumbles Easily

As termites consume wood, they create a network of tunnels and chambers within it. This weakens the wood to the point where it can easily crumble or disintegrate when pressure is applied. Wood that feels soft, spongy, or breaks apart easily is a strong indicator of significant termite damage. This is often seen in areas where the wood has been consistently exposed to moisture, making it more appealing to termites. Examples include damaged porch posts, deck railings, or structural beams in crawl spaces. The extent of the crumbling is often directly related to the duration and severity of the infestation. If you discover any wooden elements of your home that exhibit this type of damage, it's imperative to consult with a termite professional immediately. This level of damage suggests a long-standing infestation that has likely compromised the structural integrity of the affected area. Our comprehensive inspection will assess the full extent of the damage and determine the best course of action for both treating the termites and addressing the structural issues. We can provide recommendations for qualified contractors who specialize in repairing termite-damaged wood. Ignoring crumbling wood can lead to further structural deterioration and potentially dangerous conditions. Early intervention can minimize the scope of necessary repairs and protect the long-term stability of your home. We understand the emotional impact of discovering significant termite damage.

Pinpoint Holes in Drywall

While termites primarily feed on wood, they can sometimes tunnel through drywall to access other food sources or create exit points. Small, pinpoint holes in drywall, often accompanied by a fine powdery substance around the holes, can be a sign of termite activity. These holes are typically very small and can be easily overlooked. The powdery substance is often drywall dust created by the termites as they bore through the material. While not as common as other signs, pinpoint holes in drywall should not be dismissed, especially if other signs of termites are also present. It indicates termite activity within the wall cavity, which could affect the wooden framing behind the drywall. Our inspection process includes a careful examination of walls and ceilings for these subtle indicators of termite presence. Using specialized tools, we can determine if the holes were caused by termites and assess the extent of their activity behind the drywall. Treatment will then focus on eliminating the colony within the wall structure and preventing further damage to the drywall and framing. Repairing the holes can be done once the termite issue has been resolved. Recognizing these seemingly minor signs is crucial for early detection and prevention of more widespread damage. Homeowners should inspect their walls regularly for any unusual changes in appearance.

Stuck Windows or Doors

Termite activity can cause wooden window and door frames to swell or warp, making them difficult to open or close. This is often a result of the termites consuming the wood within the frames and the associated moisture they introduce. The structural integrity of the frame is compromised, leading to its distortion. You might notice that windows or doors that previously operated smoothly now stick or require force to open or close. While this can sometimes be attributed to humidity or settling, when combined with other potential termite signs, it warrants investigation. Termite damage to window and door frames can also lead to air leaks and decreased energy efficiency. Our inspection will specifically examine window and door frames for signs of termite activity and associated damage. We can assess the cause of the sticking and determine if termites are responsible. If termites are present, our treatment plan will address the infestation within the frames and surrounding structures. Repair or replacement of the damaged frames may be necessary depending on the severity of the infestation. Consulting a professional is essential to determine the underlying cause of stuck windows or doors, as it could be an early warning sign of a significant termite problem. Addressing this issue promptly can prevent further damage and maintain the functionality and security of your windows and doors.
